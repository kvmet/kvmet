# Poly(amory)

I have been poly for a long time,
but I don't usually talk about it.
Now, I am finally in a place where I
feel that I need to discuss it with
my family so this is where I am going
to write out my thoughts.

## How do I "come out"?

This is tough really. I think what
I will have to do is discuss with
my parents individually. I considered
trying to find resources to share
with them online, but I think that
they will most likely appreciate
having something that comes directly
from me and that is in my own words.

Also, I really don't like much of the
"short-form" explainers available
online. I feel like they can get too
into the minutia and broad details
about how non-monogamous relationships
function in a way that would distract
from my ability to describe my own
relationships effectively.

There are many, many different
structures for relationships and I
worry that offering all of those up
will be overwhelming for people who
are not as familiar with the concepts.

For these reasons, I think it will be
best for me to write up my own version
and to present it as additional reading
alongside my actual, direct discussions
with my family.

## Why now?

I have been poly for a long time. My
family has even met partners before
that I was poly with, but I just never
had multiple long-term relationships
at the same time that I felt needed to
be shared with them.

Now, I am in that situation and I want
to give them the opportunity to love
my partners and to understand my
relationships better. I do expect there
will be some friction during this
process but I trust my family and I
know that they will love me regardless
even if they don't fully understand
my decisions and motivations.

## Do your partners know?

Of course. In-fact my partners are also
poly.

potential questions:

Here are common questions family members often ask about polyamory, with brief answers:

1. "Isn't this just cheating?"
- No, polyamory is based on honesty and consent from all involved parties
- Everyone knows about and agrees to the arrangement
- There are clear boundaries and communication

2. "Don't you get jealous?"
- Jealousy can occur, but it's managed through communication
- Many poly people experience "compersion" (joy at their partner's happiness)
- Healthy poly relationships address emotions openly

3. "What about the children?"
- Many poly families raise healthy, well-adjusted children
- Children benefit from having multiple loving adults in their lives
- Open communication and age-appropriate honesty is key

4. "Is this just a phase?"
- For most poly people, this is their genuine relationship orientation
- Many maintain successful long-term poly relationships
- It's not about being unable to commit

5. "What will people think?"
- Privacy boundaries can be set according to comfort levels
- Many poly people are selective about who they tell
- Social acceptance is growing

6. "Isn't this morally wrong?"
- Ethical non-monogamy is based on consent and honesty
- Different cultures have different relationship models
- What matters is that all involved are consenting adults

7. "How does this work practically?"
- Scheduling and communication tools
- Clear boundaries and agreements
- Regular check-ins with all partners

8. "What about STIs/sexual health?"
- Regular testing
- Open communication about health
- Safe sex practices and agreements

9. "Won't someone always feel left out?"
- Time management is important
- Different relationships can fulfill different needs
- Quality time is prioritized with all partners

10. "What happens if you want to get married?"
- Some poly people choose not to marry
- Others have ceremonial commitments with multiple partners
- Legal marriage is possible with one partner while maintaining other relationships
